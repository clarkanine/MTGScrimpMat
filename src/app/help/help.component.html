MTG Scrimp Help

So you want to stream paper MTG with OBS?

You've come to the right place! When I first started watching Star City Games or Channel Fireball or other pro MTG tournaments, I loved how crisp the layout looked. A buddy of mine at a local card shop decided to run his own stream. I played in the local tournaments, watching the stream from the sidelines and noticed it was very...bland. He literally had a webcam on a 5" tripod pointing at a table. No visuals on the stream either. He had to remind his audience of the life totals and play by plays orally. I saw room for improvement, and thus, MTG Scrimp was born!

Setup

Since MTG Scrimp is young, there are a lot of features that can be improved. But for the time being, there are some relatively simple configurations you'll need/want in order to stream paper MTG effectively. First off, please use MTG Scrimp on Google Chrome. It is the browser I developed MTG Scrimp for and also happens to be my browser of choice.

The text produced for your stream is going to be saved to files locally. Hopefully, OBS will end up supporting reading text from URLs and not just files, but for the meantime, we'll make do. Some helpful configurations will be enabling Google Chrome to download multiple files at once. Also, specifying a new place for Google Chrome downloads to go will make organizing your text files easier. Note that if you go to another site in the Chrome Browser and download something, it will also go to that folder. Chrome easily lets you change your download folder.

Setting up OBS

After configuring your Google Chrome download settings, the first thing you're going to want to do is navigate to the 'Current Game' page. You can do this from the menu on the left side of the screen or by pressing 3 horizontal lines menu button.

Once there, go ahead and enter some values in both of the player forms and then press the 'Update Player' button underneath each of the forms. This might start the download automatically, or if you've just setup the ability to download multiple files, you may be asked by Chrome if you'd like to allow this. Select whatever option pops up that lets you download multiple files at once.

This next part is the most time consuming and I'm hopeful that I can make it easier in the future. Go into OBS and create 'Text' sources for all of the values you want to display on screen (i.e. the fields available to change on the 'Current Game' page). Name them something meaningful like 'player1life' or 'right_player_name' or whatever your favorite naming convention is. Next, change the properties on each of these text to be able to 'Read from file'. On Mac, you can access these properties by right clicking and selecting 'Properties' or by selecting the source and clicking the gear icon. Once you've clicked the 'Read from file' checkbox, browse for the associated file that you just downloaded from MTG Scrimp.

You've now got everything setup! Move the values in OBS to wherever you'd like them to be on the screen! Maybe you even have a nice overlay to put them on. Now you can make changes within MTG Scrimp, and pressing 'Update Player' will save those changes which will then be seen by OBS.
